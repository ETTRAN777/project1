About Us:
Mojang Studios is a Swedish video game developer best known for creating Minecraft, one of the best-selling and most influential games of all time. Founded in 2009 by Markus "Notch" Persson, the company quickly gained worldwide recognition for its sandbox-style gameplay that allows players to build, explore, and survive in a blocky, procedurally generated world. Microsoft acquired Mojang in 2014 for $2.5 billion, and the studio continues to develop Minecraft while expanding its universe with spin-offs like Minecraft Dungeons and Minecraft Legends. Mojang is renowned for its commitment to community-driven development and regular game updates.

Role Overview:
As a Software Engineer at Mojang Studios, you will be at the forefront of developing and enhancing the world-renowned Minecraft franchise and other projects within the Mojang ecosystem. This role requires a deep passion for game development, a strong technical background, and the ability to work within a collaborative and innovative environment. You will contribute to building new features, improving performance, ensuring game stability, and refining core gameplay mechanics to deliver high-quality experiences to millions of players worldwide. Key Responsibilities: Game Development & Feature Implementation Design, develop, and implement new features for Minecraft and other Mojang titles across multiple platforms (PC, consoles, mobile, and cloud-based services). Enhance core gameplay mechanics, ensuring they align with the vision and creativity that define Mojang Studios. Work closely with designers, artists, and other engineers to bring new ideas and concepts to life. Contribute to expanding and refining Mojang’s proprietary game engine, ensuring optimal performance and compatibility. Optimization & Performance Enhancement Improve game performance and optimize rendering, networking, and AI components to provide smooth gameplay experiences across various devices. Identify and resolve performance bottlenecks, memory leaks, and latency issues. Ensure efficient use of resources to support large-scale multiplayer environments. Technical Leadership & Collaboration Participate in architectural discussions, contribute to system design decisions, and provide insights into best practices for scalable and maintainable code. Conduct code reviews to maintain high-quality coding standards and mentor junior engineers. Collaborate with teams across Microsoft Gaming to integrate new technologies and services where applicable. Bug Fixing & Quality Assurance Diagnose, troubleshoot, and resolve bugs in a timely manner while maintaining stability and minimizing disruptions for players. Work closely with QA teams to test and validate new features and updates. Develop automated tests and tools to streamline the testing process and ensure high code quality. Live Service & Continuous Updates Contribute to Mojang’s continuous delivery model, supporting frequent game updates, patches, and content expansions. Monitor and respond to live game telemetry, player feedback, and community reports to identify and resolve critical issues. Skills & Qualifications: Technical Skills Proficiency in Java and/or C++, with strong experience in game development. Experience with game engines, rendering pipelines, and performance optimization techniques. Understanding of multiplayer game architecture, networking, and backend services. Familiarity with scripting languages such as Python or JavaScript for automation and tool development. Experience working with Unity, Unreal Engine, or custom game engines is a plus. Knowledge of cloud services such as Azure PlayFab, AWS, or similar for game backend infrastructure. Soft Skills Strong problem-solving abilities and analytical thinking. Ability to work effectively in a team-oriented, creative, and fast-paced environment. Excellent communication skills, with the ability to convey complex technical concepts to non-technical team members. Passion for gaming, particularly Minecraft, and an understanding of its community-driven approach. Preferred Experience: 3+ years of professional experience in software engineering, preferably in the game industry. Experience developing and shipping commercial games, especially those with large player bases. Understanding of modding tools, APIs, and community-driven content integration. Prior experience in game security, anti-cheat systems, or user-generated content moderation is a bonus.
